⚠️ CRITICAL INTELLIGENCE

This recipe contains anchovies. Before you close this tab — they DISSOLVE completely. You will not taste fish. It's the same trick behind Caesar dressing and puttanesca. I wouldn't lie to you about pasta.

However, if the anchovy intel is a dealbreaker, we have Option B: Soy Chorizo standing by. Your call.

THE SECRET WEAPON: Gochujang — a Korean fermented chili paste that makes this unlike any vodka pasta you've ever had. It's sweet, spicy, deeply savory, and the reason Josh's recipe went viral. Mixed with Mutti's double-concentrated tomato paste (Italy's #1 brand, uses 5.5 lbs of real tomatoes per pound of paste), it creates a glossy rust-colored sauce that coats every ridge of the rigatoni. It's genuinely insane.

THE SCREENPLAY

A recipe in seven acts

Choose Your Fighter
OPTION A — Anchovy Fillets (recommended): 4 oil-packed fillets dissolve completely into olive oil in 90 seconds. Zero fishy taste. Pure umami. The Italian way.

OPTION B — Soy Chorizo: 3 oz crumbled, browned until crispy (~4 min). Gives you the smoky, spicy, textural bits. Remove to a plate, build sauce in the flavored oil, fold back in at the end.

The Cast of Characters

3½ Tbsp extra virgin olive oil · 4 anchovy fillets OR 3 oz soy chorizo · 1½ red Fresno chilies, finely chopped · 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced · 2 tsp sugar · 2 Tbsp gochujang · ⅔ cup Mutti Double Concentrated tomato paste · 2 tsp vodka · 3 Tbsp water · 1 cup heavy cream · 10 oz Afeltra rigatoni · ½ cup finely grated Pecorino Romano · 1½ Tbsp cold unsalted butter, cubed · Kosher salt · Parmigiano Reggiano + black pepper for the finale

Act I
The Boiling Point
Fill a large pot halfway with water. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Salt it until it tastes like the ocean. This is your only chance to season the pasta from the inside. Don't mess this up. The entire dish is counting on you.
Act II
The Protein Awakens
Option A: Olive oil + anchovies in a large pan over medium-low. Press with a wooden spoon, stirring constantly until they dissolve completely. They should melt, not fry. This creates a deeply savory oil base.

Option B: Olive oil over medium heat. Add crumbled soy chorizo. Cook until browned and crispy. Remove crispy bits to a plate (they'll return for the finale). Keep the gorgeous flavored oil in the pan.
⏱ 1–4 MIN
Act III
The Foundation
Medium heat. Add chopped Fresnos + sliced garlic + a pinch of salt. Sauté 1 minute until fragrant and softened. Stir in sugar, then add the gochujang and tomato paste. Now cook 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens, darkens slightly, and starts caramelizing and sticking to the pan. That sticky stuff on the bottom? That's called fond. It's the flavor foundation of the entire dish. Don't rush it. Don't burn it. Respect the fond.
⏱ 3–4 MIN
Act IV
The Rigatoni Enters
Drop the pasta into the boiling water. Cook to al dente per package directions (12–14 minutes for Afeltra). Before draining, ladle out ¾ cup of starchy pasta water and set it aside. This cloudy liquid is gold — it's what emulsifies the sauce into that glossy, clingy coating later.
⏱ 12–14 MIN
Act V
The Deglaze
Splash vodka and water into the sauce pan. Scrape up every bit of caramelized fond with a wooden spoon — this is where the magic lives. Pour in the heavy cream, bring to a boil over medium-high, then immediately reduce to low. Simmer 1–2 minutes until slightly thickened. Taste and adjust salt.
⏱ 2 MIN
Act VI
The Marriage
Add drained rigatoni directly to the sauce. Kill the heat completely. Now vigorously stir in the grated pecorino and cold cubed butter — the residual heat will melt them into a glossy, emulsified coating that clings to every ridge. If the sauce is too tight, add reserved pasta water one tablespoon at a time (go easy). If doing Option B, fold those crispy soy chorizo bits back in now. Final taste for salt.
Act VII — Finale
The Grand Finale
Divide into warm bowls (run them under hot water for 30 seconds). Finish with a generous shower of freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano and several cracks of black pepper. Serve immediately — this sauce tightens as it sits.

Pour the wine. Clink glasses. Enjoy.
